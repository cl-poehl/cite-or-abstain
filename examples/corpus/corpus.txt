§4.2.1 — Active surveillance criteria (synthetic)

Active surveillance is the preferred management approach for ISUP grade group 1
(Gleason 6) prostate cancer in patients with life expectancy greater than ten
years. For ISUP grade group 2 (favorable intermediate-risk) disease, active
surveillance may be considered in selected patients with favorable features
including low PSA density and limited tumor volume; however, definitive therapy
is more commonly recommended at this risk level.

§5.1.3 — PSMA-PET in initial staging (synthetic)

PSMA-PET imaging is recommended for initial staging of unfavorable
intermediate-risk and high-risk localized prostate cancer prior to definitive
therapy. Its role in low-risk and favorable intermediate-risk disease is more
limited and should be considered case-by-case.

§6.4.2 — Systemic therapy at metastatic diagnosis (synthetic)

In patients with newly diagnosed metastatic hormone-sensitive prostate cancer
(mHSPC), intensified systemic therapy at the time of metastatic diagnosis
improves overall survival compared with androgen deprivation therapy (ADT)
monotherapy. Options for intensification include the addition of docetaxel
chemotherapy or of a novel hormonal agent (abiraterone, apalutamide,
enzalutamide, or darolutamide combined with docetaxel). Selection should account
for patient fitness, comorbidity burden, and patient preference.

§7.3.1 — nmCRPC (synthetic)

For patients with non-metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (nmCRPC)
and PSA doubling time of ten months or less, the addition of an
androgen-receptor pathway inhibitor (apalutamide, darolutamide, or enzalutamide)
to ongoing androgen deprivation therapy is recommended. Metastasis-free survival
benefits have been demonstrated in the SPARTAN (apalutamide), ARAMIS
(darolutamide), and PROSPER (enzalutamide) trials.

§8.2.4 — High-risk localized disease (synthetic)

For high-risk localized prostate cancer, both definitive external beam
radiotherapy with long-course androgen deprivation and radical prostatectomy
with extended pelvic lymph node dissection are recommended options. Choice
should be individualized based on patient factors and shared decision making.
